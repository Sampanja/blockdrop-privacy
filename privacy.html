This Privacy Policy explains how information is handled when you use Block Drop (the "Game"), a mobile game published by [INSERT PUBLISHER NAME] ("we," "us," or "our") for Android devices. By downloading or using the Game, you agree to the practices described below. If you do not agree, please do not use the Game.

1. Summary

Block Drop does not require an account and does not ask you for personal information such as your name, email address, or phone number. The Game stores your scores and settings only on your own device. The Game shows advertisements through Google AdMob, and AdMob automatically collects certain technical data to provide those ads. This policy explains what that means.

2. Information Stored On Your Device Only

Your gameplay data — including high scores, selected settings, and unlocked items — is saved locally on your device. We do not collect this data, upload it, or store it on any server we control. If you uninstall the Game or clear its data through your device settings, this information is permanently deleted.

3. Information Collected Through Advertising (Google AdMob)

To keep the Game free, we display ads using Google AdMob, a service provided by Google. When ads are served, the Google Mobile Ads SDK automatically collects and processes certain data. According to Google, this includes:

  • Device and account identifiers, including the Android Advertising ID;
  • IP address (used to infer approximate, country- or city-level location);
  • Device information, such as device model and operating system version;
  • User interactions with ads (for example, ad impressions and clicks);
  • Diagnostic information, such as crash and performance data.

This data is collected and used by Google to deliver and measure ads, prevent fraud, and (where you have consented) show personalized ads. We do not control how Google collects or uses this data. Google's handling of this data is governed by the Google Privacy Policy. You can also review how Google uses data from apps that use its services at policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites.

4. How This Data Is Used

The technical data described in Section 3 is used by Google AdMob to:

  • Deliver advertisements within the Game;
  • Limit how often you see the same ad and measure ad performance;
  • Detect and prevent fraud and abuse;
  • Show personalized ads, where you have given consent.

5. Your Consent Choices

The first time you open the Game, if you are in a region where consent is required (such as the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland), you will be shown a consent message through Google's User Messaging Platform. There you can choose whether to allow personalized advertising.

You can change your choice at any time within the Game by selecting [INSERT METHOD — e.g., "Settings → Privacy Options"].

You can also limit ad personalization on your device:

  • Android: Settings → Google → Ads, then choose "Delete advertising ID" or "Opt out of Ads Personalization."

Opting out does not remove ads; it means the ads you see will no longer be tailored to your interests.

6. Data Retention

We do not retain any of your data ourselves, because your gameplay data never leaves your device. Any technical or advertising data collected through Google AdMob is retained by Google according to its own retention policies. Please refer to the Google Privacy Policy for details.

7. Children

Block Drop is not directed to children under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided personal information through the Game, please contact us using the details in Section 11 and we will take appropriate steps.

8. Your Rights (GDPR, UK, and Similar Laws)

If you are located in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or another region with similar data protection laws, you may have rights to access, correct, or delete personal data, and to object to or restrict certain processing.

Because we do not hold your data on our own servers, you can delete all data we are associated with simply by uninstalling the Game. For data processed by Google AdMob, those rights are handled by Google; please see the Google Privacy Policy. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

9. California Residents

We do not sell your personal information. Block Drop does not collect personal information beyond the technical advertising data described in this policy. If you have questions about your rights under California law, you may contact us using the details in Section 11.
